Transformer Neural Networks

The foundation of modern deep learning models in NLP and beyond.

What is a Transformer?

The Transformer is a deep learning architecture introduced in the paper "Attention Is All You Need" (2017). It revolutionized natural language processing by enabling parallelization and long-range dependency modeling through self-attention mechanisms instead of recurrence.

Core Components

  • Multi-Head Self-Attention: Allows the model to focus on different parts of a sequence in parallel.
  • Positional Encoding: Injects sequence order information into the input embeddings.
  • Feedforward Layers: Applied after attention to add non-linearity and depth.
  • Layer Normalization & Residual Connections: Help stabilize training and preserve gradients.

Why Transformers?

Unlike RNNs, Transformers process all tokens at once, which leads to faster training and better utilization of parallel hardware like GPUs. They also excel in capturing contextual relationships in language.

Applications of Transformers

  • Machine translation (e.g., Google Translate)
  • Text summarization
  • Question answering (e.g., ChatGPT, BERT)
  • Vision Transformers (ViT) for image classification
